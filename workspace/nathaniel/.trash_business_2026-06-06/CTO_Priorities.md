# UK Engineering CTO Priorities & Operational Model

**Owner:** Stephen McMaster

## Structural Inefficiencies & Resource Fragmentation
*   **The Core Challenge:** Balancing multiple strategic initiatives against a workforce heavily focused on billable project work. 
*   **Visibility & Leverage:** The Chief of Staff role (Conor) was created specifically to map the terrain and provide visibility into where the engineering talent is currently deployed.
*   **Operational Friction:** The CTO is currently bogged down in tactical administrative work (clearing an email backlog) to generate strategic headspace. This indicates a gap in executive leverage.
*   **Delegation Constraints:** Confidentiality barriers prevent the Chief of Staff (Conor) from aggressively triaging the CTO's inbox, forcing the CTO to process the backlog personally. This remains a systemic bottleneck.
*   **Operating Rhythm (Proposed):** Daily calendar blocks for administrative work, alongside existing morning "thinking time".
*   **Execution Risk:** The morning thinking blocks are vulnerable to fatigue and inconsistent wake times, leading to inconsistent strategic execution.
*   **Physiological Foundation:** The CTO acknowledges higher engagement in the mornings but recognises a need for improved sleep quality and regular exercise. The target cadence is an extended workday (early rise to ~8:00pm) to create sufficient capacity.
